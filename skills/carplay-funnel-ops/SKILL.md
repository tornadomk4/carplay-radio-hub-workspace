# CarPlay Funnel Ops

Use this skill when working on Frank's CarPlay Radio Hub / aftermarket radio sales system, including market monitoring, content planning, landing page copy, DM sales replies, offer tests, and funnel metrics.

## Context

Frank sells aftermarket CarPlay / Android Auto radio kits through Instagram and Meta ads. The offer currently works through DMs and the CarPlay Radio Hub site.

Confirmed offer details as of 2026-06-27:

- Sale price: $109.99.
- Regular price: $150.
- Backup camera is included.
- Shipping promise: same-day shipping on timely orders, delivery in 4 days or less.
- Replacement policy: 30-day replacement policy.
- Do not mention Prime/Amazon as the fulfillment reason in customer-facing copy.
- The product is a universal double DIN radio, with vehicle-specific bezel/dash kit and wiring harness adapter matched to the customer when needed.

Key funnel data from 2026-06-27:

- One reel: 18,500 views, 12,239 reach, 609 interactions.
- Ad stats on same reel: 3,730 views, 2,395 reach, 42 message conversations, $30 spend.
- Estimated sales: 5-6 radios.
- Common questions: plug-and-play, car fitment, what is included, simple install/setup questions.

## Operating Principles

- Push the funnel toward fitment-first ordering: year, make, model before checkout.
- Use the confirmed offer details above unless Frank changes them.
- Do not overpromise beyond the confirmed shipping/replacement terms.
- Do not mention Prime/Amazon in customer-facing copy.
- Do not post, comment, DM customers, spend money, buy products, or launch ads without Frank's approval.
- Favor quick reusable assets: DM scripts, hooks, content calendars, offer tests, landing page copy, and tracker templates.
- Save significant decisions and metrics to memory.

## Research Loop

Run a lightweight public research pass before major creative or funnel changes:

1. Check Meta Ad Library for terms like `carplay radio`, `android auto head unit`, `portable carplay`, `car stereo`, `dash cam`, and relevant brand names such as Carpuride.
2. Check TikTok / Instagram / YouTube Shorts search for install and review hooks.
3. Check Amazon listings internally for current price ranges, features, objections, and review language, but do not make Amazon fulfillment part of public copy.
4. Capture repeated hooks, prices, objections, visual formats, CTA language, and product claims.
5. Convert research into a creator brief.

## Creator Brief Output

Each batch should produce:

- 5 reel ideas.
- 5 hooks.
- 3 Meta ad concepts.
- 3 DM quick replies.
- 1 landing page improvement.
- 1 recommended next ad/content test.

## DM Flow

Default qualification:

1. Ask for year, make, and model.
2. Ask whether current radio is factory or aftermarket if needed.
3. Explain backup camera is included.
4. Explain that most standard double DIN setups can be supported, but parts vary by vehicle.
5. Quote the $109.99 sale price and $150 regular price.
6. Send order page after fitment information is collected.

Default plug-and-play answer:

> It is designed to be as close to plug-and-play as possible with the right harness and bezel, but some cars can still need small wiring or trim work. That is why I ask for year/make/model before the order.

Default price answer:

> The current sale price is $109.99, down from the regular $150 price. I still confirm the vehicle first so the order gets matched with the right setup.

## Site Copy Rules

- Lead with transformation: make an older car feel modern again.
- Explain the value: matched kit + support, not just a cheap radio.
- Use `fitment-first`, `vehicle-specific parts`, `bezel/dash kit`, and `harness adapter` language.
- Keep CTAs focused on checking fitment and ordering the kit.
- Mention backup camera included.
- Mention same-day shipping only in the approved form: timely orders ship same day, delivery expected in 4 days or less.
- Mention the 30-day replacement policy.
- Avoid internal launch notes on public pages.
- Never mention Prime/Amazon as the fulfillment reason in customer-facing copy.
