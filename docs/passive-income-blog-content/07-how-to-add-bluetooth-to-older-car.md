# How to Add Bluetooth to an Older Car (2026 Guide)

**Meta Title:** How to Add Bluetooth to an Older Car — 5 Easy Methods (2026)
**Meta Description:** Learn 5 ways to add Bluetooth to an older car. From FM transmitters to full CarPlay radio upgrades, find the best option for your budget.
**Target Keywords:** how to add bluetooth to older car, bluetooth for old car, car bluetooth upgrade

---

If your car was built before 2010, there's a good chance it doesn't have Bluetooth. Maybe you've been aux-cording your music and squinting at your phone for directions. There are five proven ways to add Bluetooth to an older car, ranging from $10 to $150.

## Method 1: FM Transmitter ($10-25)

The cheapest option. Plugs into your cigarette lighter and broadcasts audio to an empty FM station.

**Pros:** Cheap, universal, no installation
**Cons:** Audio quality suffers from FM interference, no microphone for calls, looks messy

**Best for:** Temporary solution, rental cars, ultra-budget

## Method 2: Bluetooth Aux Adapter ($15-30)

Plugs into your aux input (if your car has one). Pairs with your phone for wireless audio.

**Pros:** Better audio quality than FM, simple setup, portable
**Cons:** Only works if you have an aux input, no hands-free calling on most models

**Best for:** Cars with aux inputs that just need wireless audio

## Method 3: Bluetooth Cassette Adapter ($15-25)

For cars with cassette players. Inserts like a normal tape but streams Bluetooth audio.

**Pros:** Surprisingly good audio, no wires hanging, retro-cool
**Cons:** Only works in cassette-equipped cars, can jam if the mechanism is worn

**Best for:** Cars from the 90s/early 2000s with working cassette decks

## Method 4: Bluetooth-Enabled Aftermarket Radio ($50-150)

Replace the factory radio with a modern Bluetooth head unit. This is where CarPlay radios shine — they include Bluetooth plus navigation, backup cameras, and app integration.

**Pros:** Best audio quality, hands-free calling, modern features, backup camera support
**Cons:** Requires installation, higher upfront cost

**Best for:** Anyone who wants a permanent, high-quality upgrade

## Method 5: Factory Bluetooth Integration Module ($50-200)

Some cars can accept a factory-style Bluetooth module that integrates with the existing radio. This maintains the stock look.

**Pros:** Factory appearance, steering wheel control retention
**Cons:** Limited to specific vehicles, less feature-rich than aftermarket

**Best for:** Purists who want to maintain the factory look

## Our Recommendation

For most people, Method 4 (aftermarket CarPlay radio) is the best value. You get Bluetooth audio, hands-free calling, wireless CarPlay/Android Auto, a backup camera, and a modern touchscreen — all for $109-150.

The audio quality improvement alone is worth it. Factory radios from the 2000s were designed for CDs and FM — they weren't built for streaming high-quality audio from your phone.

## What to Look For
- **Bluetooth 5.0+** for better range and audio quality
- **External microphone** for clearer calls (built-in mics sound terrible)
- **Wireless CarPlay/Android Auto** so you don't need to plug in your phone
- **Backup camera input** for added safety
