# Best CarPlay Radios for Honda Civic (2001-2021)

The Honda Civic is one of the most popular cars in America — and one of the best candidates for a CarPlay upgrade. Whether you drive a 7th-gen coupe from the early 2000s or a 10th-gen sedan from 2016, a modern CarPlay radio transforms the driving experience.

## Why the Civic is Perfect for CarPlay

**Double DIN opening.** Most Civics from 2001 onward use a double DIN radio opening, which means modern touchscreen radios fit without major dash modification.

**Huge aftermarket support.** Because the Civic is so popular, there are dash kits, wiring harnesses, and trim pieces available for virtually every generation. Parts are affordable and easy to find.

**Strong enthusiast community.** If you run into any install questions, there are thousands of Civic owners who've done the same upgrade. Forums, YouTube tutorials, and Reddit threads cover every generation.

## Best CarPlay Radio for Each Civic Generation

### 6th Gen (1996-2000)
These older Civics have a single DIN opening, so you'll need a dash kit to fit a double DIN radio. The factory radio is showing its age — a CarPlay upgrade is transformative for these cars.

### 7th Gen (2001-2005)
The 7th-gen Civic was one of the first to offer a double DIN opening in trim levels with the premium radio. A CarPlay radio fits cleanly with a basic dash kit. The factory amplifier in some models needs a bypass harness.

### 8th Gen (2006-2011)
This generation has a distinctive two-tier dash design. The upper display shows climate info and odometer readings. A CarPlay radio replaces the lower section, and the upper display continues to function normally. Specific 8th-gen dash kits are available.

### 9th Gen (2012-2015)
The 9th-gen Civic has a similar two-tier setup. Factory navigation was available on EX-L and above trims. Replacing it with CarPlay gives you modern navigation and app integration. The install is straightforward with the right kit.

### 10th Gen (2016-2021)
The 10th-gen Civic came with Honda's Display Audio system on higher trims, which supports CarPlay from the factory. If your Civic has the base 5-inch radio, upgrading to a full CarPlay touchscreen is a huge improvement.

## What to Look For

**Wireless CarPlay.** If you have an iPhone, wireless CarPlay means your phone connects automatically when you get in the car. No cables needed.

**Android Auto support.** Even if you drive a Civic with an iPhone now, Android Auto support means the radio works if you switch phones later.

**Backup camera input.** Most Civics from this era didn't have a factory backup camera. Adding one with your new radio is a major safety improvement.

**Steering wheel control retention.** The right kit maintains your steering wheel audio controls. This is important for safety and convenience.

## Installation Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Most Civic owners can handle the install in 1-2 hours with basic tools. The factory radio is held in by four bolts and a wiring harness. With the right dash kit and harness adapter, it's a clean swap.

If you're not comfortable with DIY, a local car audio shop can install a CarPlay radio in a Civic for $75-150.

## The Bottom Line

The Honda Civic is one of the easiest and most rewarding cars for a CarPlay upgrade. Parts are affordable, the install is well-documented, and the improvement to daily driving is massive.
